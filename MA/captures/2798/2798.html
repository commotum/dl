Introduction
?

In day-to-day life, we use informal language that is linguistically more expressive but may lack the rigor needed in formal mathematics.

For instance, consider the following example of a sentence in informal language:

The weather is fine.

Notice that this statement is a bit vague:

  • The weather is fine where? It could be fine in some places and not so much in others.

  • What is meant by "fine"? Some people like sunny weather, while others prefer less hot, cloudy days.

In contrast, formal language, as seen in mathematical proofs, follows strict syntax (rules for constructing sentences) and semantics (rules for interpreting sentences).
