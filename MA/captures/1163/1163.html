Imagine that we have two factors that influence the size of the deer population:

  • Unconstrained, the rate at which the deer population increases is proportional to the size of the deer population (i.e., exponential growth).

  • However, as the deer population increases, food resources become more scarce. The forest cannot sustain an arbitrarily large number of deer. Therefore, we must specify the maximum number of deer that the forest can sustain. This maximum number is the forest's carrying capacity, denoted as before.
