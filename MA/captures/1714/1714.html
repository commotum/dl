We now count the number of sequenced out-trees on vertices using a different method.

Suppose we initiate a rooted forest on vertices with no edges. Each vertex is a rooted tree, and our forest has rooted trees.

Then, we construct a sequenced out-tree edge-by-edge, adding the edge-labels sequentially (i.e., followed by followed by etc).

Suppose we have already added edges to our tree, which means our forest currently contains rooted trees.
