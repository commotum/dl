Uniqueness of the Diagonalization
?

Given a matrix the diagonalization

is almost unique, but not quite.

When we construct the diagonal matrix we can put the eigenvalues in any order. So this matrix is unique up to permutations of the order of the eigenvalues. Note that by swapping the eigenvalues in we then have to swap the corresponding eigenvectors (columns) in

Also, since any non-zero multiple of an eigenvector is an eigenvector as well, the eigenvector basis of can consist of these multiples instead. So the invertible matrix is unique up to multiples of its columns.
