Introduction

A mixture is any substance made up of two other substances. We usually think of one of the substances as being mixed into the other, like salt in water.

Two numbers describe a mixture:

  • the total amount of the mixture itself (in liters, grams, pounds, etc.), and

  • the percentage of the mixed-in part.

We can multiply these two numbers to find the total amount of the mixed-in part:
