Introduction

In this lesson, we discuss a concept analogous to center of mass that applies to rotating bodies.

Recall that the center of mass of a plane lamina is defined as the point in the coordinate plane where the entire mass of the lamina could be concentrated to produce the same total moment about the coordinate axes as the original laminar.

Analogously, the radius of gyration of a plane lamina is the distance from an axis of rotation that the entire mass of the lamina would need to be concentrated to produce the same moment of inertia as the original lamina.
