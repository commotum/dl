A Note on Differentiability
?

A surface is differentiable at a point if and only if it admits a unique tangent plane at Intuitively, this means that, in the neighborhood of the surface can be approximated as a plane.

Note that this is analogous to the case of functions of one variable. A function is differentiable at a point if and only if it admits a unique tangent line at This is the same as saying that, in the neighborhood of the curve can be approximated as a straight line.

We'll explore these ideas in more depth at the end of the lesson. For now, let's get some practice at computing tangent planes.
