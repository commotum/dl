Continuous Partial Derivatives Imply Continuity
?

For a multivariable function, existence of the gradient implies continuity. More precisely, given a function and a point if exists, then is continuous at

However, remember that the gradient only exists if the partial derivatives and both exist and are continuous at

Therefore, if and both exist and are continuous at then is continuous at

Watch out! The continuity of the partial derivatives is absolutely crucial in order to imply continuity. The existence of the partial derivatives alone is not enough to imply that a function is continuous at a point.
