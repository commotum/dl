The scatter plot below compares data on the number of home runs and the annual salary of several baseball players.

Our scatter plot shows a correlation between the number of home runs and the annual salary , which suggests the following:

An increase in the number of home runs causes an increase in salary.

In this instance, this sounds like a reasonable thing. We can visualize this situation as follows:

To better understand relationships like this, we must first understand the difference between correlation and causation:

  • Correlation means a relationship/pattern exists between the outputs of two events.

  • Causation means that one event causes the other event to occur.

However, correlation is not causation! Let's consider another example that illustrates this.

Reverse Causation
?

Suppose there is a correlation between the number of people who wear medical face masks in public and the number of seasonal flu cases .

Although there is a clear correlation, this does not mean that an increase in the number of face masks causes an increase in the number of flu cases

The situation is quite the opposite:

An increase in the number the flu cases causes an increase in the number of people wearing face masks in public.

We can visualize this statement as follows:

This scenario is called a reverse causation. So, we always should be careful about what causes what.

The connection between correlation and causation is generally more complicated than it seems. Let's see one more example.

Example: Causation vs. Outside Causations
?

There is a correlation between "the outside temperature is cold" and "people buy more canned soup." Which of the following statements is most likely true?

  1. An increase in canned soup sales causes a decrease in temperature.
  2. A decrease in temperature causes people to buy more canned soups.
  3. More people indoors causes the temperature to drop, and people buy more canned soups.
EXPLANATION

Correlation is not causation:

  • Correlation means a relationship/pattern exists between the outputs of two events.

  • Causation means that one event causes the other event to occur.

People eat more soups to keep warm when the weather is colder, which suggests the following:

"A decrease in temperature causes people to buy more canned soups."

Therefore, the correct answer is "II only."

Coincidence
?

When looking for causation between two variables, it could be the case that there is no reasonable connection between them. In this case, we say that the correlation is coincidental. This can be visualized as follows:

For example, suppose the number of hot dogs sold at a baseball game correlated with the number of runs scored by the team What kind of causation is this?

Well, the number of hot dogs sold shouldn't influence the number of runs scored by the team. Similarly, the number of runs shouldn't affect hot dog sales. So, neither causes nor causes The correlation is coincidental.

Example: Identifying Causation vs. Coincidence
?

There is a correlation between the number of people purchasing cool drinks in a store and the number of people buying newspapers. Which of the following statements is most likely true?

  1. More people purchasing newspapers causes more people to buy cool drinks.
  2. More people purchasing cool drinks causes more people to buy newspapers.
  3. The correlation between the number of people purchasing cool drinks and newspapers is coincidental.
EXPLANATION

Correlation is not causation:

  • Correlation means a relationship/pattern exists between the outputs of two events.

  • Causation means that one event causes the other event to occur.

Purchasing cool drinks does not cause more people to buy newspapers, and buying newspapers does not cause people to buy cool drinks, which suggests the following:

"The correlation between purchasing cool drinks and newspapers is coincidental."

Therefore, the correct answer is "III only."

Example: Identifying Causation vs. Multiple Causations
?

The above scatter plot shows the correspondence between the ball possession percentage per game and the number of wins of teams in the NBA. Which of the following is most likely true?

  1. A higher percentage of ball possession is the only cause of an increase in the number of wins.
  2. A higher percentage of ball possession causes a decrease in the number of wins.
  3. A higher percentage of both ball possession and point shot attempts are factors that influence the number of wins.
EXPLANATION

The scatter plot shows that a team's ball possession and the number of wins correlate. But correlation is not causation:

  • Correlation means a relationship/pattern exists between the outputs of two events.

  • Causation means that one event causes the other event to occur.

A higher percentage of ball possession causes a team to lose fewer games. But the number of point shot attempts also influences the number of wins, which suggests the following:

"A higher percentage of both ball possession and point shot attempts are factors that influence the number of wins."

Therefore, the correct answer is "III only."
