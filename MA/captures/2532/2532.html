Connection Between Phase Lines and Slope Fields
?

Below is the slope field for the autonomous differential equation

Notice that for autonomous differential equations, the slope marks are invariant horizontally; that is, any two points with the same -coordinate have the same slope.

Therefore, drawing more than one vertical line of slope marks is redundant, since each vertical line is identical.

Instead of drawing the full slope field, we can represent all the necessary information on a single vertical line. This is the phase line.

The diagram below shows how the slope field for

corresponds to its phase line.

The direction of the arrows on the phase line is determined by the sign of :

  • If on an interval, the slopes are positive (), and solutions increase. The arrow on the phase line points up.

  • If on an interval, the slopes are negative (), and solutions decrease. The arrow on the phase line points down.
