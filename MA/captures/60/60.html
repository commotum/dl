Introduction
?

A compound statement is a sentence that consists of two or more statements separated by logical connectors, such as the word "and" or the word "or."

For example, the following sentence is a compound statement:

is an integer and is positive

The first statement is " is an integer," and the second is " is positive." These two statements are separated by the logical connector "and."

The following sentence is also a compound statement:

is odd or is even

In this case, the first statement is " is odd," and the second statement is " is even." These two statements are separated by the logical connector "or."

We can express the logical connectives "and" and "or" using symbols:

  • The word "and" is represented by the symbol "". Notice that it resembles "A" for "And."

  • The word "or" is represented by the symbol "".

Let's translate the following sentence into symbolic form:

is an integer and is positive

To start, we introduce the letters and to denote each individual statement:

  • is an integer

  • is positive

Then, our sentence becomes

that is,

Finally, note the following terminology:

  • The statement " and " is called the conjunction of the statements and

  • The statement " or " is called the disjunction of the statements and
