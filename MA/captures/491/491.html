Introduction
?

Any transformation that maps a polygon to a similar polygon is called a similarity transformation.

Any combination of transformations made up of translation, rotation, reflection, and dilation will result in a similarity transformation.

To understand why this is true, remember:

  • Rigid motions (rotation, reflection, and translation) do not change the lengths or angles of the original figure. They map polygons to congruent polygons. If two polygons are congruent, then they are similar.

  • A dilation with scale factor multiplies all the lengths of the original shape by (leaving the angle measures unchanged), thereby producing a similar shape.
