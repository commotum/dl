There is some nice intuition to these conditions.

  • The first condition states that must be nonnegative everywhere.

  • The third condition states that we compute the probability of an event by summing (integrating) over all regions associated with

It's worth taking a minute to compare these conditions with those we've seen previously. Firstly, they are the continuous analog of those for the joint PMF of two discrete random variables. Secondly, they are the two-dimensional analog of those for the PDF of a (single) continuous random variable

It's also worth noting that these conditions can be generalized to multivariate distributions of continuous random variables.
