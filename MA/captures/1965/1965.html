Dimensions of Eigenspaces
?

In general, given an eigenvalue of a matrix we can have the following two situations:

  • If is a simple root of the characteristic equation, then the eigenspace has dimension

  • If is not a simple root of the characteristic equation (it's a double, triple, quadruple root, etc.), the dimension of the eigenspace equals the number of free variables in the system of linear equations corresponding to

Watch out! The second rule does not claim that if an eigenvalue is, say, a double root, then It says that we have to examine the corresponding linear system and only then make the conclusion depending on the number of free variables.

However, there is one thing we can determine about the dimension of the eigenspace, and it is given by the following theorem.

If an eigenvalue is a root of multiplicity in the corresponding characteristic equation, then the eigenspace has dimension not greater than

Recall that the multiplicity of an eigenvalue as a root of the characteristic equation is called algebraic multiplicity. On the other hand, the dimension of the corresponding eigenspace is called the geometric multiplicity of the eigenvalue. Our theorem above states the following:

The geometric multiplicity of an eigenvalue is always less than or equal to the algebraic multiplicity.
