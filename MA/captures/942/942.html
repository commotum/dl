EXPLANATION

Recall that polynomials have the following closure properties:

  • Polynomials are closed under addition, subtraction, and multiplication. This means that the sum, difference, and product of two polynomials is always a polynomial.

  • Polynomials are not closed under division. This means that the quotient of two polynomials is not always a polynomial.

With that in mind, let's examine our expressions in turn.

  • Expression I is a polynomial. Even though polynomials are not closed under division, the quotient of these two polynomials results in a polynomial:

  • Expression II is not a polynomial. Carrying out the division, we get which is not a polynomial.

  • Expression III is a polynomial. It's a product of two polynomials, and polynomials are closed under multiplication.

Therefore, the correct answer is "I and III only."
