We have learned how to factor quadratics. However, we can also factor higher-degree expressions, such as cubics. When factoring a cubic, the goal is exactly the same: we want to transform it into a product of two or three binomials.

To factor a cubic, we can often factor by grouping. To demonstrate, let's use this technique to factor the following cubic:

Step 1: Group the two larger terms and the two smaller terms.

Step 2: Factor the two larger terms and the two smaller terms separately, so that the leftover binomials inside the parentheses match. Here, we factor out from the two larger terms and from the two smaller terms, and the leftover expressions match.

Step 3: Rearrange the expression so that the binomial factors in parentheses appear at the front of each of the two terms.

Step 4: Factor out the binomial in parentheses from the entire expression.

Note: Just like with quadratics, not all cubics can be factored. However, of the cubics that can be factored, many can be factored by grouping.
