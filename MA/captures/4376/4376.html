Note the following:

  • The proof contains no details about how a suitable is found. For simple sequences, this is usually worked out separately and the details are omitted from the formal proof.

    However, if a sequence is more complicated and finding a suitable is non-trivial, you might consider including some of the details of how is found in the formal proof.

  • Notice that we skipped a few steps in the algebra when writing the full proof. This is quite normal. Formal proofs usually only include the steps essential to understanding the core argument. Although, this depends on the author and the intended reader. If the intended readers are new to mathematical proofs, keeping more detail is probably a good idea.
