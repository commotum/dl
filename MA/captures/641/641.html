Trigonometry problems often occur in the natural world and everyday circumstances. To effectively model problems using trigonometry, it's helpful to introduce some terminology.

Suppose that an observer is standing at some distance from an object they wish to observe.

  • An angle of elevation is the angle formed by the observer between their horizontal line of sight and the line of sight with the object when the object lies above the horizontal line of sight.

In both of the above examples, we also say that the observer and the object make an angle of with the horizontal. Whether the object is above or below the horizontal line of sight is usually determined by the context.
